# 10 - Host-Side Systems and SRE Economics

## Scope

- Study admission control, queueing, warmup, fragmentation, routing, retries, cancellation, p95 or p99 control, and cost per token.
- Treat service behavior outside the model as first-class inference engineering work.

## Notes

Use `notes/` for SLO design, capacity planning, error budgets, routing policy, and cost-model assumptions.

## Experiments

Use `experiments/` for queue simulations, concurrency sweeps, SLO tuning, and cost-sensitivity studies.

## Exit Criteria

- You can defend an SLO, a capacity plan, and a cost plan.
- Host-side bottlenecks are explained with measurement, not intuition.
